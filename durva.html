Madhubani painting is a traditional Indian art form that originated in the Mithila region of Bihar, India. It's also known as Mithila art.

Madhubani paintings often depict scenes from Hindu mythology, festivals, weddings, or local life.

Madhubani paintings were traditionally done on freshly plastered mud walls and floors of huts, but today they can be found on paper, cloth, and canvas
